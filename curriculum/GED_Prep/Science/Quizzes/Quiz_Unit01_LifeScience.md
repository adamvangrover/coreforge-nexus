# Quiz: Unit 1 - Life Science

## Instructions
Answer the following multiple-choice and short-answer questions. Circle the best answer.

### Multiple Choice

1.  **Which of the following is found in plant cells but NOT in animal cells?**
    a) Nucleus
    b) Mitochondria
    c) Cell Wall
    d) Ribosome

2.  **What is the primary function of the mitochondria?**
    a) Storing water
    b) Producing energy (ATP)
    c) Controlling the cell
    d) Photosynthesis

3.  **In a Punnett Square, a capital letter (e.g., T) represents a:**
    a) Recessive allele
    b) Dominant allele
    c) Mutation
    d) Phenotype

4.  **Mitosis results in:**
    a) Two identical daughter cells
    b) Four different sex cells
    c) One dead cell
    d) A zygote

### Short Answer

5.  **Explain the difference between genotype and phenotype. Give an example.**
    *   _________________________________________________________________________
    *   _________________________________________________________________________

6.  **Why is the nucleus often referred to as the "control center" of the cell?**
    *   _________________________________________________________________________
    *   _________________________________________________________________________

---

## Answer Key (Teacher Use Only)
1.  **C** (Cell Wall)
2.  **B** (Producing energy)
3.  **B** (Dominant allele)
4.  **A** (Two identical daughter cells)
5.  **Genotype** is the genetic code (e.g., Bb), while **Phenotype** is the physical trait (e.g., Brown eyes).
6.  Because it contains the **DNA** which holds the instructions for building and operating the cell.
