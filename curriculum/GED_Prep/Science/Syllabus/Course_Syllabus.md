# GED Science Preparation Course Syllabus

## Course Overview
This comprehensive science course is designed to prepare students for the GED® Science Test. It covers the three major content areas required by the GED Testing Service: Life Science, Physical Science, and Earth & Space Science. The curriculum emphasizes scientific practices such as reading scientific texts, designing experiments, and reasoning with data.

## Course Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1.  **Analyze Scientific Information:** Comprehend and interpret scientific texts, diagrams, and data.
2.  **Apply Scientific Methods:** Design investigations, identify variables, and evaluate experimental procedures.
3.  **Reason with Data:** Calculate statistics (mean, median, mode), determine probability, and interpret graphs.
4.  **Master Core Content:** Demonstrate understanding of Life Science (40%), Physical Science (40%), and Earth & Space Science (20%).

## 12-Week Pacing Guide

### Unit 1: Scientific Practices & Inquiry (Week 1)
**Focus:** The scientific method, experimental design, and data analysis.
*   **Week 1:**
    *   **Topic:** Introduction to Science & The Scientific Method
    *   **Lesson:** Identifying Hypotheses, Independent/Dependent Variables.
    *   **Activity:** "Pendulum Energy" Experiment (Focus on variable control).
    *   **Assessment:** Quiz 1 - Scientific Method.

### Unit 2: Life Science (Weeks 2-5)
**Focus:** Cell biology, genetics, evolution, and ecosystems. (40% of GED).
*   **Week 2:**
    *   **Topic:** Cell Structure and Function
    *   **Textbook:** Chapter 1 - Life Science: Cells & Genetics.
    *   **Lesson:** Cell Theory, Organelles (Plant vs. Animal).
    *   **Lab:** "Plant Cell Model" / Microscope Lab.
*   **Week 3:**
    *   **Topic:** Genetics and Heredity
    *   **Lesson:** DNA, RNA, Punnett Squares, Mendelian Genetics.
    *   **Activity:** Genetic Traits Survey.
*   **Week 4:**
    *   **Topic:** Evolution and Natural Selection
    *   **Lesson:** Common Ancestry, Adaptation, Evidence for Evolution.
*   **Week 5:**
    *   **Topic:** Ecosystems and Energy Flow
    *   **Lesson:** Food Webs, Carbon Cycle, Symbiosis.
    *   **Assessment:** Unit 1 Test - Life Science.

### Unit 3: Physical Science (Weeks 6-9)
**Focus:** Energy, work, motion, chemistry, and physics. (40% of GED).
*   **Week 6:**
    *   **Topic:** Energy and Work
    *   **Textbook:** Chapter 2 - Physical Science: Energy & Work.
    *   **Lesson:** Kinetic vs. Potential Energy, Conservation of Energy.
    *   **Demo:** "Photosynthesis" (Energy transformation in biology).
*   **Week 7:**
    *   **Topic:** Motion and Forces
    *   **Lesson:** Newton's Laws, Velocity, Acceleration.
    *   **Lab:** Ramp and Car Physics.
*   **Week 8:**
    *   **Topic:** Structure of Matter (Chemistry)
    *   **Lesson:** Atoms, Periodic Table, Chemical Bonds.
*   **Week 9:**
    *   **Topic:** Chemical Reactions
    *   **Lesson:** Balancing Equations, Endothermic/Exothermic reactions.
    *   **Assessment:** Unit 2 Test - Physical Science.

### Unit 4: Earth and Space Science (Weeks 10-11)
**Focus:** Earth's systems, plate tectonics, weather, and the solar system. (20% of GED).
*   **Week 10:**
    *   **Topic:** Earth's Systems and Climate
    *   **Textbook:** Chapter 3 - Earth & Space: Systems & Climate.
    *   **Lesson:** Water Cycle, Atmosphere, Climate Change.
*   **Week 11:**
    *   **Topic:** The Solar System and Universe
    *   **Lesson:** Planets, Stars, Galaxies, Big Bang Theory.
    *   **Assessment:** Unit 3 Test - Earth & Space Science.

### Review and Final Exam (Week 12)
*   **Week 12:**
    *   **Review:** Comprehensive review of all three units.
    *   **Strategies:** Test-taking strategies for GED Science.
    *   **Assessment:** Final Practice Exam.

## Instructional Strategies (UDL & ELL Support)
*   **Visual Aids:** Use of diagrams for cell structures and atomic models to support ELL students.
*   **Hands-on Learning:** Weekly labs to reinforce abstract concepts with concrete experience.
*   **Vocabulary Support:** Key terms (e.g., *mitosis*, *velocity*, *hypothesis*) provided with definitions and sentence stems.
*   **Differentiation:** Advanced students can design their own experiments; students needing support use guided lab templates.
