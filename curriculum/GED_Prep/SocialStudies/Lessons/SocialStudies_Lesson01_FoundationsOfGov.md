# Lesson Plan: Foundations of American Government

## Lesson Overview
Students will explore the historical roots of the American government, focusing on the Declaration of Independence and the Constitution. They will understand the transition from the Articles of Confederation to the current federal system.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1.  Explain the purpose of the Declaration of Independence.
2.  Identify the weaknesses of the Articles of Confederation.
3.  Describe the key compromises made during the Constitutional Convention (e.g., Great Compromise, 3/5 Compromise).

## Materials Needed
*   Textbook Chapter 1.1
*   Copies of the Declaration of Independence and Preamble to the Constitution.
*   "Articles of Confederation vs. Constitution" comparison chart.

## Lesson Procedure

### 1. Introduction (10 min)
*   **Hook**: Ask students, "If you could start a new country, what are three rules you would make sure everyone followed?"
*   Discuss the concept of a "Social Contract".

### 2. Direct Instruction (20 min)
*   Lecture on the timeline: Colonial Period -> Revolution -> Articles of Confederation (Failure) -> Constitution.
*   Explain the concepts of *Federalism* and *Separation of Powers*.

### 3. Guided Practice (15 min)
*   **Activity**: Break students into small groups. Give each group a scenario (e.g., "The federal government wants to tax the states").
*   Ask them to determine if this was allowed under the Articles of Confederation vs. the Constitution.

### 4. Independent Practice (10 min)
*   Students write a short paragraph explaining why the framers felt a stronger central government was necessary.

### 5. Assessment & Wrap-up (5 min)
*   **Exit Ticket**: List one strength and one weakness of the Articles of Confederation.
