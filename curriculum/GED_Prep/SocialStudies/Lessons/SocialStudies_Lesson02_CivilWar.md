# Lesson Plan: Causes and Effects of the Civil War

## Lesson Overview
This lesson covers the primary causes of the US Civil War, the major turning points, and the lasting impact of Reconstruction.

## Learning Objectives
1.  Identify the role of slavery and states' rights in the outbreak of the war.
2.  Analyze the significance of the Emancipation Proclamation and the Gettysburg Address.
3.  Understand the 13th, 14th, and 15th Amendments.

## Materials Needed
*   Textbook Chapter 2.2
*   Map of Union and Confederate states.
*   Text of the Gettysburg Address.

## Lesson Procedure

### 1. Introduction (10 min)
*   Show a map of the US in 1860. Ask students to identify differences between North (Industrial) and South (Agrarian).

### 2. Direct Instruction (20 min)
*   Discuss the Missouri Compromise and the Kansas-Nebraska Act.
*   Explain the election of Abraham Lincoln and the subsequent secession of Southern states.
*   Outline the war: Fort Sumter, Antietam, Gettysburg, Appomattox.

### 3. Primary Source Analysis (20 min)
*   Read the **Gettysburg Address** together.
*   Discussion questions: What does Lincoln mean by "a new birth of freedom"? How does he redefine the war's purpose?

### 4. Assessment (10 min)
*   Short quiz on key terms: Secession, Confederacy, Union, Emancipation.
