# GED Social Studies Course Syllabus

## Course Overview
This 12-week comprehensive course is designed to prepare students for the GED Social Studies test. The curriculum focuses on four major content areas: Civics and Government (50%), U.S. History (20%), Economics (15%), and Geography and the World (15%). Students will develop skills in reading for meaning in social studies, analyzing historical events and arguments, and using numbers and graphs in a social studies context.

## Course Goals & Learning Objectives
By the end of this course, students will be able to:
1.  Analyze and evaluate the development of American constitutional democracy.
2.  Understand fundamental economic concepts and systems.
3.  Interpret and analyze historical events and their impact on the present.
4.  Apply geographic concepts to analyze relationships between humans and the environment.
5.  Distinguish between fact and opinion, and evaluate the credibility of sources.

## Resources
- **Textbook**: CoreForge GED Social Studies Textbook
- **Primary Sources**: The U.S. Constitution, Declaration of Independence, Federalist Papers, Landmark Supreme Court Cases.
- **Tools**: Interactive maps, economic simulators.

## Grading Policy
- **Quizzes**: 20%
- **Unit Tests**: 30%
- **Lesson Assignments**: 25%
- **Final Exam**: 25%

## Differentiation & Accommodations
- **UDL**: Text-to-speech options, video summaries of historical events, graphic organizers for economic concepts.
- **ELL Support**: Bilingual glossaries for civics terminology, sentence stems for document analysis (e.g., "The author argues that...").
- **IDEA**: Extended time on assessments, simplified text options, alternative response formats.
