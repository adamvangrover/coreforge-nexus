# Unit 1 Test: Civics and Government

**Time**: 60 Minutes
**Total Points**: 50

## Part 1: Multiple Choice (20 points)
1.  Who is the Commander-in-Chief of the military?
    a) Speaker of the House
    b) Chief Justice
    c) President
    d) Secretary of Defense

2.  How many Senators does each state have?
    a) 1
    b) 2
    c) Based on population
    d) 4

3.  The "Great Compromise" settled the issue of:
    a) Slavery
    b) Representation in Congress
    c) Taxation
    d) The location of the capital

4.  Which amendment guarantees the right to a speedy trial?
    a) 1st
    b) 2nd
    c) 4th
    d) 6th

(Additional questions would follow...)

## Part 2: Short Answer (15 points)
5.  Describe the process for a bill to become a law.
6.  Explain the significance of the court case *Marbury v. Madison*.
7.  What are interest groups and how do they influence politics?

## Part 3: Essay (15 points)
**Prompt**: "The United States Constitution has been called a 'living document'. Explain what this means and provide examples of how the Constitution has adapted to changing times through amendments or judicial interpretation."

*   **Criteria**:
    *   Clear thesis statement.
    *   Use of specific examples (e.g., 19th Amendment, Civil Rights).
    *   Logical organization.
