# Chapter 2: U.S. History

## 2.1 Colonial America and the Revolution
*   **1607**: Founding of Jamestown, the first permanent English settlement.
*   **1775-1783**: The Revolutionary War. Colonists fought for independence from Britain due to "taxation without representation".

## 2.2 Westward Expansion and the Civil War
*   **Manifest Destiny**: The belief that the US was destined to expand across North America.
*   **The Civil War (1861-1865)**: Fought between the North (Union) and South (Confederacy) primarily over the issue of slavery and states' rights.
    *   **Emancipation Proclamation**: Issued by Lincoln, declaring slaves in rebel states free.
    *   **Result**: Union victory, preservation of the US, abolition of slavery (13th Amendment).

## 2.3 Industrialization and Immigration
Late 19th/Early 20th Century saw massive growth in industry (railroads, steel, oil) and waves of immigration from Europe and Asia, leading to urbanization.

## 2.4 The World Wars
*   **World War I (1914-1918)**: US entered in 1917.
*   **The Great Depression (1929-1939)**: Economic collapse. FDR's **New Deal** expanded government role in the economy.
*   **World War II (1939-1945)**: US entered after Pearl Harbor. Fought against Axis powers (Germany, Japan, Italy).
    *   **The Cold War (1947-1991)**: Ideological tension between US (Capitalism) and USSR (Communism).

## 2.5 Civil Rights Movement
1950s-60s movement to end racial segregation and discrimination. Key figures: MLK Jr., Rosa Parks.
*   **Civil Rights Act of 1964**: Outlawed discrimination.
