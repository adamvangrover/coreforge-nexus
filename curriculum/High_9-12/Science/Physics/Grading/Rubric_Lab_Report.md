# Physics Lab Report Grading Rubric

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Proficient (3) | Developing (2) | Beginning (1) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Purpose & Hypothesis** | Purpose clearly stated; Hypothesis is testable and scientifically grounded. | Purpose stated; Hypothesis is testable but lacks detailed reasoning. | Purpose unclear; Hypothesis is vague or not testable. | Missing Purpose or Hypothesis. |
| **Data Collection** | Data is organized in labeled tables with units. Multiple trials performed. Precision is appropriate. | Data in tables with units. Some trials performed. | Data is disorganized or lacks units. Only single trials. | Data is missing or illegible. |
| **Analysis & Graphing** | Graphs are titled, labeled (axes/units), and scaled correctly. Calculations are shown and correct. | Graphs have minor errors (e.g., missing title). Calculations mostly correct. | Graphs are missing labels or scales are poor. Calculation errors. | Graphs missing or incorrect. No calculations. |
| **Conclusion** | Conclusion directly answers the purpose, cites specific data as evidence, and addresses sources of error. | Conclusion answers purpose and cites some data. Mentions error briefly. | Conclusion does not cite data or fails to address purpose. | Conclusion missing. |
| **Formatting** | Neat, professional, correct grammar/spelling. Follows specified format. | Generally neat, minor errors. | Messy or hard to follow. Many errors. | Illegible or fails to follow format. |
