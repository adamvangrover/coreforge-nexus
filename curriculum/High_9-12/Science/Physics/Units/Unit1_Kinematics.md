# Unit 1: Kinematics - Describing Motion

## Unit Overview
This unit introduces students to the language of motion. We will move from qualitative descriptions to precise mathematical definitions of position, displacement, speed, velocity, and acceleration. Students will learn to represent motion using words, diagrams, numbers, graphs, and equations. This unit lays the foundation for all future study of mechanics.

## Learning Objectives
By the end of this unit, students will be able to:
1.  **Differentiate** between scalar and vector quantities (distance vs. displacement, speed vs. velocity).
2.  **Calculate** average speed, average velocity, and acceleration.
3.  **Apply** the four kinematic equations to solve problems involving constant acceleration in one dimension.
4.  **Interpret and Construct** position-time, velocity-time, and acceleration-time graphs.
5.  **Analyze** free-fall motion as a special case of constant acceleration.

## Essential Questions
*   How do we describe where an object is and how it is moving?
*   What is the difference between "how fast" and "how fast in a specific direction"?
*   How can graphs tell the story of an object's motion?

## Instructional Sequence (Summary)
1.  **Introduction to Measurement & 1D Motion:** Position, Distance, Displacement.
2.  **Speed vs. Velocity:** The importance of direction.
3.  **Graphing Motion (Part 1):** Position-time graphs. slope = velocity.
4.  **Acceleration:** Definition and conceptual understanding.
5.  **Graphing Motion (Part 2):** Velocity-time graphs. slope = acceleration, area = displacement.
6.  **Kinematic Equations:** Derivation and problem solving.
7.  **Free Fall:** Gravity as acceleration.
